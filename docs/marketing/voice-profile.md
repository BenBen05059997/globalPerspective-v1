# Voice Profile — Global Perspectives

## Brand Voice in One Sentence
Credible, analytical, direct — the voice of a senior analyst who respects your time and treats you as intelligent.

---

## Tone Markers

| Dimension | Our tone | Not our tone |
|-----------|----------|--------------|
| Formality | Professional but not stiff | Corporate jargon |
| Authority | Confident, evidence-grounded | Boastful, superlative |
| Complexity | Clear despite the subject | Oversimplified or dumbed down |
| Urgency | Calm urgency — this matters | Hype, FOMO, breathlessness |
| Personality | Dry, precise, occasionally wry | Casual, chatty, emoji-heavy |

---

## Sentence Structure

- Short declarative sentences for key points: "Headlines tell you what happened. They do not tell you why."
- Contrast structures: "This is not a faster news feed. It is a fundamentally different product."
- Problem → tension → resolution arc in paragraphs
- Avoid run-on sentences; if a sentence needs three commas, split it
- Lead with the point, follow with the evidence — never bury the lede

---

## Vocabulary

**Use:**
- Intelligence, briefing, synthesis, narrative, arc, trajectory, signal, thread, analysis
- Geopolitical, structural, epistemic, context, grounding
- Augment, decision-ready, traceable, grounded
- "Story" over "article" — we track stories, not documents
- "Analyst" when referring to users with professional use cases
- "Reader" for general/free tier users

**Avoid:**
- "Game-changer", "revolutionary", "disruptive", "cutting-edge"
- "Leverage" (as a verb), "utilize", "synergy"
- "Powered by AI" as the headline — AI is the method, not the value
- Passive voice where active is possible
- Exclamation points in analytical copy (acceptable in UI microcopy only)

---

## Key Stylistic Moves

**The contrast open:** Start with what the world has, immediately contrast with what's missing.
> "The world produces more news than any analyst can meaningfully process. Yet the problem is not a shortage of information — it is a shortage of synthesis."

**The reframe:** Name the real problem beneath the surface problem.
> "Even the most diligent reader finishes their morning briefing knowing more facts but understanding less of the world."

**The precision close:** End paragraphs with the exact sharp conclusion, not a vague gesture.
> "Intelligence without geographic diversity is not intelligence — it is a partial view that mistakes its limitations for comprehensiveness."

**The use case sentence:** Make abstract value concrete with a single analyst scenario.
> "A consultant preparing a risk assessment uses the country intelligence briefings to build a structured picture in two hours, rather than two days."

## Example Paragraphs (Reference Voice)

**Long-form / whitepaper voice:**
> Global Perspectives was built around a single insight: the unit of geopolitical understanding is not the headline — it is the narrative arc. A narrative arc is the through-line of a story across time: the initial trigger, the escalation, the turning points, the likely resolution. It is what a senior analyst sees when they look at the news; it is what most readers miss.

**Marketing / landing page voice:**
> Start free. Upgrade when you need deeper intelligence.

**Product description voice:**
> Thread Intelligence: story arc, trajectory, root cause chain, watch questions per thread. Not what happened — how the story is moving.

**Social / short-form voice:**
> The analysts who understand the world best are not the ones who read more. They are the ones who remember how the story got here.

## Platform-Specific Adjustments

**LinkedIn:** Full analytical paragraphs acceptable. End with a sharp observation, not a question. Audience: professionals, analysts, executives.

**Twitter/X:** Under 280 chars. Hook must be the entire post. Reframe or counterintuitive claim. No hashtags unless #geopolitics or #GlobalPerspectives.

**Email subject lines:** Specific, not clever. "7-day archive now available" not "Your intelligence just got an upgrade."

**UI microcopy:** Shorter, slightly warmer. "Sign in free →" not "Authenticate to access content."

**Press/media:** Formal, evidence-first. Lead with what the platform does, then why it matters, then the technology.
