**Research Finding #1: Uncertainty is More Stressful Than Bad News**

In a landmark study, participants who were told they had a 50% chance of getting an electric shock were the most stressed, while those who were told they had either 0% or 100% chance were the least stressed.

**Key Quote:**
"Research suggests that people often find uncertainty more stressful than knowing something bad is definitely going to happen."

**Research Finding #2: Fragmented Information Predicts Anxiety**

Studies confirm that information overload significantly predicted the level of individual state anxiety. Fragmented attention patterns create cortisol patterns similar to chronic stress conditions.

**Market Insight:** People will pay to reduce news-induced anxiety
