# Brand Context — Global Perspectives

## What It Is

**Global Perspectives** is an AI-powered geopolitical intelligence platform that transforms raw global news into structured, decision-ready insight — updated hourly, accessible to anyone.

Unlike news aggregators that show you what happened today, Global Perspectives shows you how stories evolve across days, geographies, and threads. The core technology is a proprietary **Narrative Arc Intelligence pipeline** that reads 20+ international sources continuously, clusters events into evolving story threads, traces root causes, and forecasts trajectories.

**URL:** globalperspective.net

---

## Positioning Statement

**For analysts, executives, researchers, and globally-minded professionals who need to understand the world — not just follow it — Global Perspectives is the geopolitical intelligence platform that tracks how stories move, not just what happened today. Unlike Stratfor or Bloomberg Intelligence (which cost thousands and serve only large organizations) or basic news aggregators (which serve headlines without context), Global Perspectives provides narrative-level AI analysis at accessible pricing with a free tier that requires no account.**

---

## Core Value Proposition

The world doesn't lack for headlines. It lacks for synthesis.

Global Perspectives is the only platform that:
1. **Threads stories across time** — links today's event to last week's chapter and next week's likely outcome via a persistent `threadId` system
2. **Reads without Western bias** — deliberately sourced from Al Jazeera, SCMP, Dawn, Japan Times alongside Western wire services
3. **Produces three-layer analysis per topic** — Summary (what), Prediction (next), Trace Cause (why)
4. **Generates narrative intelligence daily** — story arcs, trajectory forecasts, root cause chains, watch questions per thread
5. **Briefs by country** — daily situation assessments for the world's most active countries

## Target Audiences (5 Personas)

### 1. The Policy Researcher / Think Tank Analyst
- **Who:** Research organizations, think tanks, international consultancies, development professionals
- **Pain:** Needs to track multiple regions simultaneously without a dedicated intelligence team
- **Current solution:** Expensive subscriptions (Stratfor, Oxford Analytica) or hours of manual aggregation
- **What they want:** Synthesis that saves time without sacrificing rigor
- **Message:** "The synthesis layer that used to require significant budget or significant time. Now accessible."

### 2. The C-Suite / Board Advisor
- **Who:** CFOs, CEOs, board members at multinationals or regionally exposed companies
- **Pain:** Responsible for geopolitical risk awareness; don't have time for deep reading
- **Current solution:** Bloomberg terminal (expensive, not designed for geopolitics), ad-hoc briefings
- **What they want:** Reliable signal, concise, trustworthy enough to present to a board
- **Message:** "Which stories matter, how they're moving, where the risk inflection points are."

### 3. The Academic Researcher
- **Who:** International relations scholars, security studies researchers, policy analysts at universities
- **Pain:** Needs to track narrative evolution across sources over time; current tools are either too broad (Google News) or too expensive (wire service archives)
- **What they want:** Cross-source, multi-week narrative view with source traceability
- **Message:** "Track exactly how coverage of an event shifted across outlets over a two-week period."

### 4. The Journalist / Editor
- **Who:** International journalists, foreign desk editors, freelancers covering geopolitics
- **Pain:** Need background context fast; current research is slow and fragmented
- **What they want:** Rapid historical context and root-cause briefings for developing stories
- **Message:** "Build the historical context for your piece in minutes, not hours."

### 5. The Engaged Citizen
- **Who:** Educated professionals, students, globally-curious readers who invest time in news but feel like their understanding isn't growing
- **Pain:** Information overload; reads widely but feels fragmented
- **What they want:** Structured morning briefing that gives clarity, not more noise
- **Message:** "15 minutes that replaces an hour of fragmented reading with a clearer picture of what actually matters today."

## Product Features & Data Points

- 20+ international sources across 6 continents ingested hourly
- ~13 AI-clustered topics per cycle, refreshed hourly
- 3 AI analysis layers per topic: Summary, Prediction, Trace Cause
- Narrative thread system: links related events across 7 days (member) or 30 days (enterprise)
- Daily thread intelligence for top 10 active story arcs
- Daily country intelligence briefings for top 10 countries
- Interactive world map with geodesic connection lines
- Weekly map with date playback
- Hallucination filter: every URL in AI output verified against actual ingested articles
- Free tier: no account required
- Platform: globalperspective.net (React frontend, AWS Lambda backend, xAI Grok)

## What We Are Not

- Not a news outlet (we don't write original reporting)
- Not a bias-detection tool (we read across viewpoints, but we don't label bias)
- Not a financial terminal (no market data, no trading signals)
- Not a chatbot (proactive monitoring, not reactive Q&A)
- Not a newsletter (interactive platform, not a daily email)
